What not to share with AI

AI tools send your input to external servers. Unless your organisation is on an enterprise-licensed, data-isolated plan, treat what you type like a public forum.

✕
Never enter client or personal information
Names, contact details, health records, Tax File Numbers, student or enrolment data — all protected under the Privacy Act and your organisation's obligations.
✕
Never paste confidential contracts, financial data, or board documents
These may be stored by the provider, used to train future models, or exposed in a breach. Use internal tools for sensitive documents.
✕
Never share passwords, API keys, or system details
Passwords, tokens, internal IP addresses, and system architecture details should never appear in an AI prompt.
✕
Never share unreleased plans, M&A activity, or sensitive strategy
Commercially sensitive information — especially anything price-sensitive for listed companies — must stay internal.
~
Be cautious with internal processes and org structure
Detailed org charts, security policies, and system architecture could help an attacker target your organisation if exposed.
